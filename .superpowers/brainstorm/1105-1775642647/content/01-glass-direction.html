Glass Design Direction

You mentioned Apple's Liquid Glass. How deep should the glass aesthetic go?

A

Glass Accents (Subtle)

Keep the current platinum noir foundation. Add glass effects to interactive elements only — buttons, cards, booking panels, navigation. The dark background stays dominant. Glass elements float on top like premium overlays.

Think: Dark luxury brand site with glass card components. Like a Porsche or Rolex site with frosted panels.
B

Glass Sections (Moderate)

Alternate between dark sections and glass-panel sections. Some full sections become translucent glass containers with depth, refraction, and layered backgrounds. Creates rhythm between solid and transparent. The "wow" factor comes from entire section panels that look like frosted glass floating over a subtle gradient or pattern.

Think: Apple's visionOS marketing site — glass panels with depth layering over dark canvas.
C

Full Glass System (Bold)

Commit fully to a glass design language. Background gradient shifts as you scroll (dark → deep blue → charcoal), and most content lives inside glass containers with real CSS refraction effects, inner shadows, and specular highlights. Every section has glass depth. Navigation, cards, panels — all glass.

Think: Apple's Liquid Glass from iOS 26 / visionOS. Full commitment to the aesthetic. Highest wow-factor but also highest risk of feeling gimmicky if not executed perfectly.

Current state: Your site already uses subtle glass on the header (backdrop-blur-md) and booking console (bg-black/55 + backdrop-blur). Option A builds on this. Options B and C would be a bigger visual shift.
