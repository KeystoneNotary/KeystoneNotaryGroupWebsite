Proposed Design Approach: Cinematic Elevation

One approach, applied to every section. The principle: if it doesn't feel like a scene in a film, it's not done yet.

The Elevation Formula

Every section below the bar gets the same treatment recipe. This is what Hero and TheFirm already do:

1. Background Depth

Large editorial watermark text, subtle gradient shifts, or atmospheric texture behind content. No section should be "just a dark box."

2. Scroll-Linked Animation

Content doesn't just "fade in" — it assembles, shifts, and layers as you scroll. Tied to scroll position (scrub), not just triggered on viewport entry.

3. Typography Scale

Headlines at editorial scale (3xl-6xl+), not cramped. Playfair Display for headlines, Inter for body. Generous tracking and leading.

4. Premium Panels

Cards and containers with multi-layer inner shadows, subtle edge highlights, and refined backdrop-blur. Not "glass" — just premium dark UI with depth.

Section-by-Section Plan

Hero & TheFirm

Keep

Minor polish only. These set the standard. Possibly tighten Hero's assurance card and refine scroll indicator timing.

Header

Elevate

Refine the glass nav treatment. Add a subtle animated border shimmer on scroll state change. Tighten mobile menu with better entrance animation. Remove "Book Appointment" CTA (booking removed) — replace with "Get in Touch" or "Request a Quote."

Credentials

Elevate

Add editorial watermark ("CERTIFIED" or "COMMISSIONED"). Scale up headline to 4xl-5xl. Credential cards get scroll-linked stagger with blur + position shifts (like TheFirm elements). Each card enters from a different direction. Add subtle parallax between card layers.

Services

Elevate

Each service gets its own "scene" — full-width presentation with large number (oversized "01" watermark), headline, and description. As you scroll, one service transitions into the next. Think editorial magazine spreads, not a numbered list. Optional: services pin and scroll horizontally.

Anti-Testimonial

New

Bold statement section. Large editorial headline: "We don't do fake reviews." Body copy explains why — you'll never see fabricated testimonials here. Instead, invite people to check your Google reviews, BBB profile, NNA directory listing. Link to real, verifiable third-party sources. The honesty IS the testimonial. Backed by a dramatic watermark and scroll-driven reveal.

FAQ

Elevate

Scale up question typography. Add editorial watermark ("ANSWERS" or "CLARITY"). Accordion items get scroll-linked entrance with directional stagger. Left info panel gets refined with premium card treatment (inner shadows, edge highlights). Potentially reduce to 5-6 most important questions.

Contact / Inquiry

Redesign

This is now the primary conversion point. Dramatic section entrance with large headline ("Let's get started." or similar). Simple 4-field form (name, email, phone, message) in a premium dark panel. Prominent phone number as an alternative. The form should feel like the culmination of the page — not an afterthought tacked on at the end.

Footer

Elevate

Tighten layout. Remove booking-related CTAs. Keep phone, email, service area, legal links. Add subtle gradient transition from Contact section into Footer so they flow together. The footer should feel like a natural close, not an abrupt stop.

Booking Section

Removed

Calendar, time picker, booking form, and price quote all removed. Backend booking API preserved but unused. Can be re-added later with a proper UX design.
